EEBug

Line Following Robot

My first year university group projct was to design and construct a small line-following robot within a specified time frame and budget. I was the team leader for this project, which provided me with valuable leadership experience and project planning skills. The robot had to follow a line drawn onto a sheet of paper, continue for bit, spiral, and then stop.

How it works

The EEBug was controlled by a microcontroller, specifically an Atmel ATTiny85. This was used as the number of microcontoller pins allowed was limited. The ATTiny was programmed using the popular Arduino platform. To detect the line, a pair of infrared light detectors were mounted on the front of the bug, to detect the intensity light reflected from the surface below by some infrared LEDs. The black line would reflefct less than the white, meaning that the robot could always move towards the black. When the line stopped, the move straight and spiral routines were triggered. A buzzer, which played small tunes at the stop and start rounded it off with a bit of flair.
